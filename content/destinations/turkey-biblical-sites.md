# Turkey - Biblical Sites & Early Christianity

Turkey is home to some of the most significant sites in early Christian history, including the Seven Churches of Revelation and locations from St. Paul's missionary journeys.

## Why Visit Turkey?

Turkey offers a unique perspective on early Christianity, with well-preserved ancient sites and a rich history of the early church. Many New Testament events took place in what is now modern Turkey.

## Key Biblical Sites

### Seven Churches of Revelation

The seven churches mentioned in Revelation:
1. **Ephesus** - Most impressive ruins, largest ancient city
2. **Smyrna** (Izmir) - Modern city, few ancient remains
3. **Pergamum** - Impressive acropolis and ruins
4. **Thyatira** - Modern city, limited ruins
5. **Sardis** - Well-preserved synagogue and temple
6. **Philadelphia** - Modern city, some ruins
7. **Laodicea** - Extensive archaeological site

### Ephesus

One of the best-preserved ancient cities:
- **Library of Celsus**: Iconic facade
- **Great Theatre**: Where Paul preached
- **Temple of Artemis**: One of the Seven Wonders
- **House of the Virgin Mary**: Traditional site

## St. Paul's Journey

Follow in St. Paul's footsteps:
- **Tarsus**: Paul's birthplace
- **Antioch**: Where followers were first called "Christians"
- **Derbe, Lystra**: Sites of Paul's missionary work

## Planning Your Trip

A comprehensive biblical tour of Turkey requires 10-14 days. Consider joining a specialized Christian tour or hiring a guide familiar with biblical history.
