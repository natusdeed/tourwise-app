# Vatican City & Rome - Christian Heritage

Rome is one of the world's most important Christian destinations, home to Vatican City, the center of the Catholic Church, and numerous sites of early Christian history.

## Why Visit Rome?

Rome offers a unique combination of ancient Christian history and modern Catholic significance. From the catacombs where early Christians worshipped to St. Peter's Basilica, the city is a living testament to Christian faith.

## Key Sites

### Vatican City

The world's smallest independent state and the heart of Catholicism:
- **St. Peter's Basilica**: The largest church in the world
- **Vatican Museums**: Home to incredible art including the Sistine Chapel
- **St. Peter's Square**: Designed by Bernini, site of Papal addresses
- **Papal Audience**: Weekly Wednesday gatherings with the Pope

### Major Basilicas

Rome has four major papal basilicas:
1. **St. Peter's Basilica** (Vatican)
2. **St. John Lateran** (Cathedral of Rome)
3. **St. Mary Major** (Largest church dedicated to Mary)
4. **St. Paul Outside the Walls** (Tomb of St. Paul)

### Early Christian Sites

- **Catacombs**: Underground burial sites of early Christians
- **Colosseum**: Where many Christians were martyred
- **Pantheon**: Converted from pagan temple to Christian church

## Planning Your Visit

Plan at least 4-5 days to fully experience Rome's Christian heritage. Book Vatican tickets well in advance, especially during peak season.
